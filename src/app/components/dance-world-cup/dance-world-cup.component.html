Dance World Cup is the leading international dancing event in which thousands of dancers from around the world participate in a variety of competitions. This competition includes 11 genres and all age groups are involved.

Since 2009, the Dance World Cup Finals have been held every year in different countries, such as Italy, France, Austria, Portugal, Romania and England. Over 12,000 dancers compete in the qualifying matches in their home countries, before they are qualified to the finals. In the Dance World Cup Final 2017, which was held in Offenburg, Germany, over 4,000 dancers representing 47 countries competed at the competition.

Last year, Hong Kong Qualifying Match was held for the first time. Over 60 dancers joined the Finals in Germany. All dancers achieved satisfactory results, with one dancer winning the bronze medal in National and Folklore Dance.

Dance World Cup is different from local competitions. Before attending the finals, dancers were required to be qualified from the qualifying match. We aim to provide an opportunity for cultural exchange between dancers. We believe “He who travels far, knows much; Those who seized the opportunity to explore, knows more.” Dancers who participated last year had a fruitful experience of acquiring stronger dancing skills, and establishing friendship with one another.

The Dance World Cup Final 2018 will be held in Spain. We look forward to your participation. See you at the event!

How to qualify for the World Finals

All dancers must qualify to take part in the Dance World Cup Finals. If there is a qualifying competition in your country of residence, you must qualify through this competition. Dancers must receive 70 marks or above at their national qualifying event to compete at the Dance World Cup Finals. Each country can enter a maximum of THREE routines per class. For more information about qualifying in your country, please contact your local qualifier organizer at the email address listed below.
